Past Candidates’ Convention Polling, Opinion Bounces

Often, well-received conventions make for big polling spikes. Sixty percent of Americans said the 1992 Democratic convention made them “more likely” to vote for Clinton, compared to 15 percent who said “less likely.” That 45-point difference came with a 16-point polling bounce after that convention. Here’s how past candidates and conventions have compared.
