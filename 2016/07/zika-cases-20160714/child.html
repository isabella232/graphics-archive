Suspected Zika Cases Swelled In February, Now Declining

Reported suspected cases of Zika virus in Latin America and the Caribbean, recorded weekly from Jan. 10, 2015, to May 21, 2016. The World Health Organization declared Zika a public health emergency on Feb. 1, 2016. Researchers expect a rebound in cases toward the end of the year during South America’s summer, but they predict the outbreak will peter out around spring of 2018.

Notes

Data for Brazil covers 10 states that report public cases. Total data excludes some countries that do not report suspected cases. A “suspected” case is diagnosed by a clinician based on symptoms; most have not been confirmed through laboratory testing.

Source: “Countering Zika in Latin America,” Science magazine
