Black Representation In Congress

The number of African-American representatives in Congress has increased. However, when compared to the African-American population size, representation is still lagging.

In 1977, African-Americans only made up 18 of Congress’ 535 members, or around 3.3 percent, though they made up 11.5 percent of the population — which would equal around 61 of 535 members. Today, there are 48 African-American members of Congress, or around 9 percent, while African-Americans make up 13.2 percent of the population. A proportional share of Congress members would be 71 out of 535.

Notes

2016 population estimates were not available from the Census Bureau; we used population estimates from 2014.
