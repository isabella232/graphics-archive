Poverty Rates

The proportion of African-Americans living below the poverty line has declined since 1977, but it remains disproportionately high. Today, more than 1 in 4 African-Americans live in poverty, compared to around 1 in 7 for the total population.

Notes

The 1977 black poverty figure is for all people classified as “black.” In 2002, the Census started separating out people who identified as “black alone” and “black alone or in combination.” Our figure for 2014 (26.2 percent) is for “black alone,” though it is not far from the figure for “black alone or in combination” (26 percent).

Source: U.S. Census Bureau
