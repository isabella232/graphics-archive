Notes

1. Delegate numbers shown are the total number available for a given state. Each state and party has its own rules for awarding delegates to candidates. Some state parties award delegates proportionally depending on the results of a caucus, primary or party convention. Other party primaries and caucuses are winner-take-all. Still others are hybrids.
2. On the Republican side, American Samoa, Colorado, Guam, North Dakota, the U.S. Virgin Islands and Wyoming will not select delegates via caucuses or primaries. In Wyoming, delegates will be selected at county and state conventions; no winner has been called yet.
3. On the Democratic side, delegate figures include both delegates awarded via the primary/caucus process and “superdelegates,” who may vote as they wish.
4. Nebraska will hold presidential primaries on May 10. However, the Democratic Party will use a separate caucus on March 5 to determine its delegate allocation.
5. Washington will hold presidential primaries on May 24. However, the Democratic Party will use a separate caucus on March 26 to determine its delegate allocation.
6. No winners have been declared in the Missouri Republican primary.
7. March 22 primary/caucus results: Arizona primary results shown are with 99.5 percent of precincts reporting. Utah Democratic caucus results are with 99.1 percent of precincts reporting. Utah Republican caucus results are with 88.7 percent of precincts reporting.

Source: Associated Press, Democratic National Committee, Republican Party, The Green Papers, CNMI Republican Party, Guam Democratic Party, State Elections Commission of Puerto Rico
