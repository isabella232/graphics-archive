How to build a “block universe”

The world we live in has three dimensions of space: up-down, forward-backward, left-right. Of course, there’s time, which appears to flow “through” space.

To make things easier, let’s imagine the world has only two dimensions of space, which would be a kind of “flat land.”

Now let’s picture 2-D Bob and 2-D Alice tossing a ball. We’ll take snapshots as the ball travels.

So now we create a 3-D space-time out of our 2-D space by adding time as a new dimension. We can stack the sequence of moments in 2-D space like a deck of cards to build this new time dimension.

Now, let’s imagine the path of the ball in the “block universe” of our 3-D space-time. Here, there is no flow of time. The ball’s entire journey exists as a single “thing” in space-time which scientists call a “world line.”

In the same way, all the events of your life from your birth to your death already exist in space-time as your own world line. The present is just a particular slice of the block, but there is nothing special about it.

In the block universe, every event in the history of the cosmos already exists. We humans perceive the flow of time, but that is just an illusion. All past and future is already there, fixed in the space-time “amber” of the block universe.

But is this really the last word on time?
