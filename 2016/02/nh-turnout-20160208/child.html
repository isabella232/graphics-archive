Voter Turnout In The New Hampshire Primary

Notes

Voter turnout is often much lower in a given party primary when there is an incumbent president of that party. (For example, the Democratic primary in 2008 — when there was no incumbent — vs. 2012, when Barack Obama, a Democrat, was the incumbent.) In 1992, Republican voter turnout may have been unusually high for an incumbent year because President George H.W. Bush was challenged by Pat Buchannan.

Source: New Hampshire Secretary of State
