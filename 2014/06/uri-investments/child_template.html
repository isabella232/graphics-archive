Painting

I bought a painting through Saatchi Online by an artist named Vladimir Kryloff from Vilnius, Lithuania. I paid $450 for the painting (it’s an abstract work called Flower Study #14) plus $139 for shipping. Total: $589. A few weeks ago I had the painting appraised by an appraiser in Washington. He set the fair market value at $900. But because it could be hard to sell the painting and because I enjoy seeing the painting on the wall at home, I’m keeping it.

  • Amount spent (2013): $589
  • Appraisal (2014): $900 (53% return)

Bulk buying

I went to Costco last year to buy household items and food in bulk as a hedge against inflation. This investment would pay off if prices were higher the following year, at least above the rate of inflation. But that didn’t turn out to be the case. The items I bought last year that I found again on my return trip this year were up only 1 percent, right around the rate of inflation. (Just in case. Comparable items: onions, pens, trash bags, dog treats, tuna, toothpaste, pistachios, Zantac, razor blades, T-shirts and Progresso soup.)
