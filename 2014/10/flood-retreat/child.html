Notes

The map shows areas where property owners have been bought out with funds from the Federal Emergency Management Agency’s Hazard Mitigation Grant Program. The program’s grants go to state and local governments to compensate property owners whose properties have lost value in major disasters. The buyouts are 75 percent funded by FEMA. Under federal law, the future use of the bought-out land is restricted to open space, recreational, and wetlands management. NPR analyzed records of more than 7,000 property acquisitions, worth more than $400 million, since 2003.
