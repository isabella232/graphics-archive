Eggs Frozen At An Earlier Age Have A Higher Chance Of Success

Probability of a live birth, based on number of eggs thawed and patient’s age at the time eggs were frozen.

Notes

— “Live birth” is defined as the delivery of one or more infants. Twins or other multiple births are counted as a single “live birth.”
— Rates shown are for eggs that were “flash-frozen” using vitrification, a process which typically results in 80 percent or more eggs surviving the thaw.

Source: Fertility and Sterility (August 2013)
