So Far, Few Attempts To Use Frozen Eggs; Fewer Births

In a “thaw cycle,” frozen eggs are thawed, fertilized and implanted. Of the eggs frozen since 2009, only a small percentage have been thawed for use. And of those attempts, 20-25 percent have resulted in a child.

Notes

— “Live birth” is defined as the delivery of one or more infants. Twins or other multiple births are counted as a single “live birth.”
— Some of the thawed eggs may have been frozen with an older method that’s largely been abandoned because of its lower success rate. Almost all eggs frozen in recent years are “flash-frozen” with vitrification, which typically results in 80 percent or more eggs surviving the thaw.

Source: Society for Assisted Reproductive Technology
