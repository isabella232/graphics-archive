Cancer Risk Factors, By State: Darker Shades, Higher Risks

Darker colors below represent a higher prevalence of factors that are associated with major risks for cancer. Darker shades represent a higher proportion of adults who smoke regularly or are considered obese. Darker shades also represent a higher proportion of residents who don't exercise regularly, or who don't consume recommended daily servings of fruits (two) and vegetables (three). For example, 21.5 percent of adults in Alabama smoke regularly and only 12.4 percent eat three servings of vegetables a day.
