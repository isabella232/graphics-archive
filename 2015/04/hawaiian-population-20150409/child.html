Measuring Hawaii’s Native Hawaiian Population

A new analysis estimates that there were far more Native Hawaiians on the island than previously thought when Capt. James Cook landed in 1778.

Notes

The new estimate, from David Swanson at the University of California, Riverside, includes only the Native Hawaiian population. In 2000 and after, the Kamehameha Schools estimates include those who are Native Hawaiian alone and in combination with other races.

Source: David Swanson (University of California, Riverside) and Kamehameha Schools, via Pew Research Center
